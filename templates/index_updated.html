About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive disorder that affects the nervous system and movement. Symptoms often start gradually and worsen over time, impacting daily activities.

Common Symptoms

  • Tremors or shaking
  • Slowed movement (bradykinesia)
  • Muscle stiffness and rigidity
  • Impaired posture and balance
  • Loss of automatic movements (e.g., blinking)
  • Changes in speech and facial expressions
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Reduced facial expressions or masked face

Risk Factors

  • Age (higher risk for older adults)
  • Genetics
  • Environmental factors, such as exposure to toxins

Prevention

While Parkinson's disease cannot be entirely prevented, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, exercising regularly, and reducing exposure to toxins may help reduce risk.
